As urban populations continue to grow, the traditional models of city planning—often heavily reliant on automobiles—are proving unsustainable. It's time for a paradigm shift.

## The 15-Minute City

One of the most exciting concepts gaining traction is the "15-minute city." This model envisions urban environments where residents can access most of their daily needs within a 15-minute walk or bike ride from their homes.

### Prioritizing Pedestrians

Streets are being redesigned to prioritize people over cars. Wider sidewalks, dedicated bike lanes, and pedestrian-only zones encourage active transportation and reduce carbon emissions.

### Mixed-Use Development

Moving away from strictly zoned commercial and residential areas, mixed-use developments blend housing, retail, and office spaces, fostering vibrant, all-day communities.

## Green Infrastructure

Modern cities must act like sponges, not funnels. Integrating parks, green roofs, and permeable pavements helps manage stormwater, reduces the urban heat island effect, and provides essential nature access for city dwellers. The cities of the future must be designed with human health and environmental resilience at the forefront.
