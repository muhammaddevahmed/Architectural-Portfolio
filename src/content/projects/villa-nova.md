# Villa Nova

## Project Overview

The concept for Villa Nova was born from the landscape itself. Perched on a gentle hillside overlooking the rolling Tuscan valley, this residence embodies the principle that architecture should emerge from its context rather than impose upon it. Our approach was to let the building become an extension of the rugged terrain — a composition of raw materials that honors the region's rich architectural heritage while embracing contemporary living.

Utilizing locally sourced stone and expansive glass panels, the boundary between interior and exterior dissolves. The villa's L-shaped configuration wraps around a central courtyard, creating a sheltered outdoor room that serves as the heart of the home throughout the Mediterranean seasons.

## Design Philosophy

Minimalism is not an absence, but a focus. We stripped away the unnecessary to reveal the essential structure, allowing natural light to paint the interiors throughout the day. Every element serves a purpose, every material speaks honestly, and every space connects intentionally to the landscape beyond.

### Core Principles

**Material Honesty**
Materials are celebrated for their inherent qualities — the warmth of oak, the texture of hand-chiseled stone, the patina of weathered brass. No material is disguised or altered beyond its natural expression.

**Light as Architecture**
The building is conceived as a light-gathering device. Deep reveals, carefully positioned openings, and reflective surfaces orchestrate a daily symphony of illumination that transforms each room from morning to night.

**Threshold as Transition**
The boundary between inside and outside is blurred through continuous floor surfaces, aligned sight lines, and the extension of interior spaces into loggias, terraces, and courtyards.

## Site & Context

### Topography

The villa occupies a south-facing slope with panoramic views of olive groves, vineyards, and the distant Apennine mountains. The design responds to the site's 15% grade through:

- **Cut-and-fill earthwork** minimizing foundation excavation
- **Stepped floor plates** following the natural contour
- **Retaining walls** crafted from excavated stone
- **Lower level** partially embedded for thermal stability

### Microclimate

The Mediterranean climate informed passive design strategies:

| Season | Strategy | Benefit |
|--------|----------|---------|
| Summer (hot, dry) | Deep overhangs, cross-ventilation, thermal mass | Passive cooling without AC |
| Winter (cool, wet) | South-facing glazing, insulated envelope | Solar gain reduces heating 60% |
| Spring/Autumn (mild) | Operable windows, outdoor rooms | Natural ventilation year-round |

### Views & Orientation

- **Primary axis** oriented to capture the most dramatic valley views
- **Secondary axis** aligned with sunrise and sunset
- **Screening elements** (stone walls, olive trees) provide privacy from neighboring properties
- **Key vistas** preserved through careful massing

## Sustainability

### Passive Strategies

| Strategy | Implementation | Energy Savings |
|----------|----------------|----------------|
| Thermal Mass | Stone walls + concrete floors | 25% reduction |
| Natural Ventilation | Cross-ventilation + stack effect | 40% cooling reduction |
| Solar Shading | Deep overhangs + deciduous trees | 50% cooling reduction |
| Insulation | Continuous exterior insulation | 60% heating reduction |

### Active Systems

**Heating & Cooling**
- Ground-source heat pump (4 boreholes at 120m depth)
- Radiant floor heating throughout
- Fan coil units for summer cooling
- Heat recovery ventilator (90% efficiency)

**Renewable Energy**
- 15 kW photovoltaic array (50 panels)
- Solar thermal for domestic hot water
- Annual production: 22,000 kWh
- Net-zero energy (annual basis)

**Water Management**
- Rainwater harvesting (20,000L cistern)
- Greywater recycling for garden irrigation
- Drip irrigation system (80% water reduction)
- Pool cover reduces evaporation by 90%

### Certifications

- **LEED Platinum** (Homes category)
- **Passive House** certification
- **WELL Gold** for occupant health
- **Living Building Challenge** (Materials petal)

## Craftsmanship & Details

### Artisanal Elements

**Stone Masonry**
- Three local stonemasons worked for 8 months
- Dry-stacked retaining walls (no mortar visible)
- Hand-chiseled finish on every exposed stone
- Traditional lime mortar for structural walls

**Timber Framing**
- Reclaimed oak trusses from 19th-century barn
- Traditional mortise and tenon joinery
- Hand-pegged connections (no metal fasteners)
- Natural linseed oil finish

**Metal Work**
- Custom bronze hardware by local blacksmith
- Hand-forged iron railings and gates
- Copper gutters with hidden fasteners
- Stainless steel cables for balcony railings

## Construction Story

### Local Resources

- **90% of materials** sourced within 100km radius
- **95% of construction waste** recycled or repurposed
- **100% of excavation stone** reused on site for retaining walls
- **Local workforce** for all trades (no imported labor)
